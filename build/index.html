Study, travel and career for Jewish youth

About the program

«Masa» is a joint project of the Israeli government and the Jewish Agency Sokhnut. More than 250 study programs (60 in Russian) and the possibility of an internship in Israel. During the development of the programs, a grant from $ 4200 to $ 8400 is provided.

Programs

General

Do you want to spend a semester or a year in Israel exploring different cultures and ideas, traditions and lifestyles while studying Hebrew on the Masa program? Learning Hebrew, one of the most ancient and unusual languages, is not only exciting, but also extremely important for successful absorption into Israeli society and for your further professional development in Israel. High-quality and effective study of Hebrew is one of the integral components of the entire educational process in Masa programs.

The Masa project provides an amazing opportunity to take an unforgettable journey to a country with a thousand-year history and a unique way of life, where you can truly experience your historical roots and feel like an integral part of the Jewish people. If you ask yourself questions: «What to do next?», «What to Study?», «Where to live?», «Where to work?» or if you just want new impressions, emotions, new friends from all over the world, then do not miss your chance to become a part of the Masa project. Learning Hebrew with the Masa project is your start to a successful future in Israel!

Academic

Spending a semester or year abroad, getting to know different cultures and ideas, traditions and lifestyle - that's what studying abroad is! Israel is not only the center of the religious world, it is also an academic center, a living laboratory of ideas and creative research.

Are you interested in studying sociology, world, jurisprudence, biology, comparative religion, law and business administration or art? Here in Israel, you can explore all this in an amazing university environment.

Internships

The Masa project offers university graduates and young professionals a wide range of programs for career advancement, professional development in various fields and internships in Israel. Choose for yourself one of the many opportunities to acquire the knowledge, skills and experience necessary for professional development in the modern world.

Find out more about professional growth, careers and internships in Israel. Get the chance to become part of a country that is a world leader in technology, business, research and environmental studies.

Volunteering

Masa offers unique opportunities for volunteering throughout Israel. You will be able to make your invaluable contribution to the development of Israeli society by working with various communities and socially vulnerable groups. Volunteering programs focus on building and strengthening communities; assistance to young people at risk; supporting the peaceful coexistence of Jews, Arabs and other local communities. All Masa volunteering programs are based on the Jewish tradition of «Tikkun Olam» (making the world better) and are guided by the principles of social responsibility and social justice.

Volunteering programs allow not only to get to know the Israeli reality, culture and traditions better, but also to bring something of their own, change the world for the better.

Religious

Masa offers a range of programs designed to deepen the connection with Jewish tradition and people. Judaism study programs range from rigorous interdisciplinary studies of the Jewish people and civilization, and study of the Torah in the best world universities to study in the best yeshivas and seminaries in Israel.

Get the opportunity to become part of the Jewish people, understand your identity, immerse yourself in the spiritual atmosphere of Jewish values, and become an experienced community worker. Your journey with Masa starts here..

How to go

To learn more about the program, contact the coordinator in your city. You will receive up-to-date information about the programs available and the cities in which you can take the program.

To be eligible for a study grant from $4200 to $8400, you must:

  1. be between 16 and 30 years old

  2. Have completed secondary education

  3. Have documentary evidence of Jewish roots
    and pass consular check

  4. For the last 2 years, be less than 3 months in a row in Israel.

If you do not qualify for these rules, you can still participate in the Masa program by paying the full cost.

FAQ

  1. Who is the organizer? Duration of programs?

    The organizer is the Masa Agency, which is a project of the Government of Israel and the Jewish Agency "Sokhnut", whose task is to support long-term programs in Israel. The goal of the project is to facilitate the annual stay in Israel of thousands of young people aged 18 to 30 from the Diaspora countries within the framework of various long-term programs lasting from 1 semester to a year.

  2. What is a Study Grant?

    The Masa Grant is the main mean through which the Jewish Agency "Sokhnut" and the Government of Israel intend to achieve the goal of the project. Masa Company is a subsidiary of the Jewish Agency, created for the implementation of projects and issuing grants (for residents of the CIS and Baltic countries) in accordance with the duration of the program and its cost.

  3. How is the cost of the program determined?

    The total cost is determined by the organizers, depending on the direction. In most cases, the Masa grant does not cover the full cost of the program, and therefore a certain amount of personal participation is provided.

  4. What is a Study Grant?

    The Masa Grant is the main mean through which the Jewish Agency "Sokhnut" and the Government of Israel intend to achieve the goal of the project. Masa Company is a subsidiary of the Jewish Agency, created for the implementation of projects and issuing grants (for residents of the CIS and Baltic countries) in accordance with the duration of the program and its cost.

  5. Is the study recognized in the project for further study?

    Universities in Israel and Western countries recognize academic studies in the framework of the Masa project if the organizer claims the academic accreditation of his program. The situation in the CIS is more complicated, and the recognition of education depends, as a rule, on the decision of each individual educational institution. Find out in advance at your school whether your studies under the Masa project will be recognized, or whether you will be able to obtain such accreditation upon your return from Israel. The Masa Project Management is not responsible for academic accreditation. Only the organizer of the curriculum is responsible for this.

  6. What the complete registration process looks like
    for the Masa program?

    Your actions: Program selection. Contact the coordinator of the selected program and register for a consular check. Filling out a questionnaire on the Masa website. Upload documents on the successful completion of consular checks on the participants' portal through your personal account. Obtaining an A2 student visa at the consulate based on a letter from Masa about the approved grant. At each stage of registration, you can contact the Masa Program Representative in Israel for assistance at masainforus@masaisrael.org
